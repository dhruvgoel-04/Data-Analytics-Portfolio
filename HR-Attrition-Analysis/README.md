# HR Employee Attrition Analysis

An end-to-end exploratory data analysis project on employee attrition using the IBM HR Analytics dataset, built using Python and Power BI.

## Overview

Employee attrition affects productivity, hiring costs, and team stability. This project explores the IBM HR dataset to identify who is leaving, what's driving it, and where the company should focus its retention efforts.

The analysis covers demographics, department and job role trends, compensation, overtime, work-life balance, job satisfaction, and tenure patterns.

## Tools Used

- Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn)
- Power BI

## Key Findings

- Overall attrition rate is ~16% — above the industry average of 10–12%
- Sales department has the highest attrition rate; Sales Representatives are the most at-risk job role
- Employees who left earned ~$2,000 less per month on average than those who stayed
- Overtime workers are ~20% more likely to leave
- Employees with bad work-life balance are twice as likely to attrition
- The 18–25 age group has the highest attrition rate across all age brackets
- Employees not promoted in 6+ years show among the highest attrition rates

## Business Recommendations

1. Reduce excessive overtime, especially in Sales and R&D
2. Review compensation for high-risk roles and departments
3. Build structured growth programs for younger employees (18–25)
4. Introduce regular promotion review cycles to address career stagnation
5. Run engagement initiatives targeting employees with low satisfaction or poor work-life balance scores
